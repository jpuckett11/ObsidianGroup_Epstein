### Why this stack is operationally significant

The standard staffing pattern in private aviation is multi-vendor. The
aircraft owner has the airframe, charter-contracts a crew through one
agency, hires cabin staff through another agency, books ground support
through a third. Three independent labor pools, three independent vetting
chains, three independent reporting accountabilities.

Pilots have ATP-level professional duties. They file flight plans, log
hours, are subject to FAA and DOT enforcement. Cabin staff are eyewitnesses
to in-flight conduct. Ground staff sees the boarding and disembarking. In a
multi-vendor staffing model, an incident on board has at minimum two or
three independent labor pools that could report it. Single-vendor crew
supply collapses that accountability structure to zero. Everyone on the
operation:

- Works for the same paymaster
- Signed the same NDA at placement
- Got vetted to the same loyalty standard
- Knows their continued income depends on the agency continuing to place
  them
- Has nobody to report to outside the agency

Aviloop offers all four staffing tiers as a single bundled service:

| Tier | Aviloop service line | Personnel supplied |
|------|------|------|
| Cockpit | "Match clients with qualified pilots to fly their aircraft" | Pilots |
| Cabin / event-side | "Other aviation professionals, including conference booth or social media influencers" | Cabin staff, model talent, brand presenters |
| Recruitment pipeline | "Set up internal flight schools for corporate clients, sourcing an aircraft and staffing" | New-entrant pilots and ground staff |
| Bespoke high-touch | "Team building events: an expert instructor will fly with one person at a time allowing them to take the controls" | Private one-on-one experience product |
| Female-specific supply | Aviatri: "find qualified professional female pilots to join their team" | Female cockpit and cabin staff |

A real aviation marketing firm sells branding work, social media campaigns,
event coverage, and content creation. It does not bundle pilot crewing with
trade-show booth staffing with cabin staff supply with internal-flight-school
operation under a single matching service. Those are not the same industry.
A real crewing agency sells pilot supply only. A real model-booking agency
sells booth and influencer supply only. A real flight school sells training.
They do not consolidate.

The combination is operationally consistent with a single-source crew-supply
service, not with the published marketing-firm framing.
