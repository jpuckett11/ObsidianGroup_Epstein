## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Leon Black, co-founder of Apollo Global Management, paid Jeffrey Epstein between **$158 million** (Dechert report figure) and **$170 million** (Senate Finance Committee revised figure) between 2012 and 2017. ## 1. WHAT DID BLACK GET FOR $170 MILLION?

### The Official Story: "Tax Advice"
Black testified and Dechert concluded that payments were for "tax, estate planning, and philanthropic advice." The actual picture is far more complex.

### A. The GRAT Transaction (~$1 Billion+ in Tax Avoidance)

**Structure:** In 2006, Black created Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs) funded with Apollo partnership interests valued at approximately **$585 million**. These were expected to grow to **$2+ billion**, creating an estimated **$1+ billion estate tax liability** if improperly structured.

**The Problem:** Black improperly received excess income from Apollo partnership interests held in the trust, assets he had told the IRS he no longer owned. This created a potentially catastrophic tax exposure.

**Epstein's "Solution":** Epstein proposed that Black provide "consideration" to the trust to cure the defect. Black has refused to disclose:
- The extent he was overpaid income from the trust
- Whether he retained voting rights on trust-held assets
- The dollar amount of Epstein's proposed "consideration"

**Payment:** Black paid Epstein **$20 million** for his role in this single transaction.

**Critical Detail:** Black's own attorneys confirmed to the Senate Finance Committee that "the idea was in the public domain and originated with Black's other legal advisors" and that Epstein merely tried to claim credit for their work.

### B. The Step-Up Basis Transaction ($600 Million Savings)

In 2015, Epstein helped structure a transaction involving loans between Black and his trusts to avoid capital gains taxes for his beneficiaries. Epstein claimed this move saved **$600 million in future gift and estate taxes**.

### C. Phaidon/Art Space Restructuring ($96-106 Million in Assets)

In 2012, Black acquired **Phaidon Press** (fine art book publisher). By October 2015, Epstein wrote that Phaidon and Artspace together had cost about **$96 million** (later adjusted to $106 million).

**What Epstein did:**
- Brad Wechsler sent Epstein detailed corporate charts showing Phaidon's ownership, governance structure, and internal money flows
- In 2015, Wechsler attached diagrams showing trusts, acquisition vehicles, and management entities
- Epstein proposed restructuring so that trusts would lend directly to Phaidon Press or fund Phaidon Global
- This created tax-advantaged structures around Black's art holdings

**Art Deal Tax Avoidance:** Epstein was also involved in a proposed **$100 million Picasso purchase from Gagosian**, advising on how art purchases could move among entities and family trusts to minimize tax exposure.

### D. The Unofficial Services: Fixer, Surveillance, Hush Money

**This is likely what the bulk of the $170M actually purchased.**

**Women Management:**
- Epstein and Brad Karp (Paul Weiss chairman) coordinated surveillance of **Guzel Ganieva** using private investigators from **Nardello & Co.**
- Karp updated Epstein on "GG's" movements, reporting she was "snuck out" of a Manhattan apartment "in a car with tinted windows" to JFK airport
- Epstein provided the location of women on Black's payroll to a "well-connected Russian government operative" and asked for "suggestions" on how to deal with them
- Epstein served as middleman for what Senator Wyden characterized as "hush money payments" to women on Black's behalf

**Payment to Ganieva (structured through lawyers):**
- $2 million at NDA signing (October 2015)
- Forgiveness of ~$1 million loan
- Monthly payments of $100,000 for 5+ years
- Total: approximately **$9.5 million**
