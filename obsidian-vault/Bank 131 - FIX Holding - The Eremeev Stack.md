# Bank 131 / FIX Holding / The Eremeev Stack

## Summary

Bank 131 is the Russian fintech bank that provided the founding capital and continuing institutional connection for [[DAY ONE VENTURES - The Active Operation|Day One Ventures]], the $450M (claimed $600M) Silicon Valley fund run by former Putin Nashi commissar Masha Drokova / Bucher. The bank's owner, Dmitry Eremeev, is the personal connector. The wider Eremeev "FIX Holding" stack (Bank 131 + Smart Glocal + ePN.bz + Tapicash) is structured to a single operational backend and a single owner, has served as Telegram's primary Russian-side payment rail since May 2022, and was placed under EU sanctions on July 19, 2025. The stack also sits inside the regional political infrastructure of US-sanctioned Tatarstan President Rustam Minnikhanov, with documented in-person engagement between Minnikhanov and Eremeev. Senator Ron Wyden's Senate Finance investigation has separately found 4,725 wire transfers totaling nearly $1.1 billion flowing through one Epstein account, with "hundreds of millions" routed via Russian correspondent bank accounts and Treasury records linking those wires to "the movement of women or girls around the world." The Eremeev stack is one of the operational targets that picture implies.

## EU Sanctions, July 19, 2025

Both Bank 131 and Smart Glocal were placed under European Union sanctions on July 19, 2025, as part of the EU's ongoing program of restrictive measures targeting Russia's financial sector and its support for the war in Ukraine.

This is a material development for the Day One Ventures inquiry because:

- Bank 131 is the documented founding investor in Day One Ventures (per Drokova / Bucher's own statements to Russian-language press and the TechCrunch profile)
- A US venture fund with a founding investor that is now an EU-sanctioned entity is exposed to the FASCSA-style supply-chain-security framework that the Director of National Intelligence first invoked against Acronis AG in October 2025 (see [[DAY ONE VENTURES - The Active Operation]] for the Acronis precedent)
- Eremeev's continuing role as "mentor and connector" to Drokova / Bucher means the relationship is not historical or arms-length; it is active

The fund itself has not been the subject of a public US enforcement action as of this writing, but the Bank 131 sanctions create the legal substrate for one.

## Telegram, Durov brothers, and the May 2022 payment-stack pivot

Following the March 2022 withdrawal of Visa, Mastercard, and Apple from the Russian payment system in response to the invasion of Ukraine, Telegram needed a Russia-side payment partner to keep Telegram Premium subscriptions and bot-driven donation flows operational for its Russian user base. In May 2022 Telegram announced the addition of two payment providers to its developer ecosystem: Bank 131 and Smart Glocal. Both were Eremeev entities.

The operational consequences:

- Bank 131 and Smart Glocal became the de facto Russian payment monopoly inside the Telegram ecosystem (~900M global users, of whom a substantial fraction are in Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, Central Asia, and the wider CIS region)
- Russian-card payments for Telegram Premium and bot-mediated donations have flowed through Bank 131 / Smart Glocal continuously since May 2022, including after the July 19, 2025 EU sanctions
- Eremeev's personal pre-existing acquaintance with the Durov brothers is the relationship that made the partnership possible. This is documented in Russian-language reporting (tginfo.me) and Russian consumer-complaint forums (vc.ru) where customers identify the Eremeev backend by name.

The Telegram payment-rail role gives Bank 131 visibility into a population of users that includes Russian state employees, military, intelligence services, dissidents, journalists, and the diaspora, by the most basic mechanism: anyone subscribing to Telegram Premium from a Russian-issued bank card transacts through Eremeev's stack.

## Wyden Senate Finance investigation: Russian correspondent bank accounts

Senator Ron Wyden's Senate Finance Committee investigation has obtained Treasury Department records documenting the financial scale of Epstein's bank activity:

- 4,725 wire transfers
- Nearly $1.1 billion in flow through one Epstein account alone (not the total)
- Additional "hundreds of millions" routed via Russian correspondent bank accounts at US institutions
- Treasury records explicitly link these wires to "the movement of women or girls around the world"
- Treasury also holds approximately 4,000 "potential lines of investigation" that Wyden alleges the Trump administration is sitting on

In March 2026, Senate Republicans blocked Wyden's "Produce Epstein Treasury Records Act," which would have compelled Treasury to surrender the underlying records to Congressional investigators.

The Eremeev stack is one of the operational instantiations the Wyden record implies. The Treasury document set will name specific correspondent banks. Bank 131's role as a Russian-side payment provider for international flows, including for Telegram and including via the Sberbank-intermediary chains identified by external Russia-sanctions analysts, makes it one of the candidate names. (The Sberbank-intermediary chain has been documented by sanctions-analyst publications, including Eurasia Review, reporting that Bank 131 worked with one of Sberbank's trusted intermediaries when Sberbank was directly cut off in 2024.)

If Wyden's Treasury record set is ever surrendered (the legislative path blocked in March 2026 may be reopened by an administration change, by FOIA litigation, or by leak), the Eremeev stack is on the candidate list of named beneficiary or correspondent institutions.

## Implications

1. **Day One Ventures is not arms-length from sanctioned Russian banking infrastructure.** Its founding investor's banking stack is now EU-sanctioned; the founding investor remains an active mentor and connector. This is the kind of LP-due-diligence concern that triggers FASCSA-style supply-chain-security review.

2. **The Telegram payment-rail role is itself an intelligence interest.** Bank 131 / Smart Glocal sit at the only chokepoint between Russian-cardholder Telegram users and the Telegram corporate stack. Anyone who controls that chokepoint has metadata visibility into a substantial fraction of the Russian-speaking world's day-to-day communications spending.

3. **The Tatarstan regional axis matches the Acronis pattern.** A US-sanctioned regional executive (Minnikhanov), a Russian-state-adjacent technology executive (Eremeev), and an export-facing Western-market fintech stack (Bank 131 / Smart Glocal). Same template as Belousov and Acronis. The pattern is now documented as a recurrent structural mechanism, not a one-off.

4. **The Wyden Treasury record set is the active US enforcement angle.** The blocked "Produce Epstein Treasury Records Act" of March 2026 is the legislative target to watch. If the underlying records are surrendered or leaked, the Eremeev stack is a candidate institution. The connection is upstream-documentable today via the public-record materials cited here.

5. **The trafficking-payments-to-Russian-banks linkage is in Treasury's own records, not inference.** Wyden states explicitly that the wire transfers were "correlated to the movement of women or girls around the world." This is the operational hinge between the financial-network thread documented in this file and the trafficking thread documented across the rest of the OWG investigation.
