## 1. The 1951 Bronfman dinner: prototype for Mega Group fundraising

In 1951, Shimon Peres at age twenty-eight was Director-General of the Israeli Defense Ministry, attempting to acquire Canadian artillery against an international arms embargo. He traveled to Montreal to ask Sam Bronfman, the Seagram patriarch, for help. Bronfman drove Peres to Ottawa in his Cadillac to meet Canadian Minister of Supply and Reconstruction C.D. Howe. Howe, impressed by Bronfman's advocacy, cut the asking price from $2 million to $1 million. When Bronfman asked Peres where the remaining million would come from, Peres said "From you." Bronfman called his wife the same day and instructed her to invite fifty people for dinner that evening. Each paid $20,000. By the end of the night, the second million was raised.

That dinner is the structural prototype for every Mega Group fundraising mechanism that followed. American Jewish billionaires raising money for Israeli defense procurement through invitation-only private gatherings, organized in hours, mediated by Peres on the Israeli side. The Mega Group, formally founded forty years later in 1991, is the institutional version of what Peres and Bronfman invented at a Montreal dinner table in 1951.
