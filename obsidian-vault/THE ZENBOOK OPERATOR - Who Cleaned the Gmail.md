## The Finnish Spotify Reset

On August 10, the day of death, someone reset Epstein's Spotify password. The reset email arrived in Finnish: "Palauta salasana."

No one in Epstein's known network is Finnish. Finland is:
- Home to strong infosec community (F-Secure, SSH Communications)
- Part of the EU but not a Five Eyes country
- Geographically between Russia and Western Europe
- Host to significant VPN infrastructure (used for anonymization)

The Finnish locale could indicate:
- A VPN exit node in Finland
- An actual Finnish person
- A device configured in Finnish for operational security
- A different operator from the ZenBook person

Two different cleaning operations, one on July 19 (Gmail), one on August 10 (Spotify), suggest at minimum two people or two sessions, possibly coordinated.

## What Google Knows

Google retains:
- Session logs (IP addresses, device fingerprints, login times)
- Activity logs (which emails were accessed, deleted, or modified)
- Recovery information (backup email, phone number)

This data can be subpoenaed. It has not been. In every other federal criminal case involving email evidence, Google session logs are standard discovery. In the Epstein case, nobody has asked.
